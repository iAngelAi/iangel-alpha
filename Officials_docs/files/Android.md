### Vous uniquementPurpose & context Fil is developing iAngel, a conversational AI assistant specifically designed for technologically vulnerable Quebec seniors. The core mission is revolutionary: to SLOW DOWN rather than accelerate AI interactions, providing "one step at a time" guidance with validation before proceeding to the next step. The target persona is "Ginette," a 72-year-old who fears fraud and technical jargon but wants to perform tasks like bank transfers without constantly asking her daughter for help. Success is measured not in revenue metrics but in achieving "one smile from someone who stopped believing in Santa Claus years ago." The project is 100% self-funded with a 10+ year vision, explicitly rejecting typical startup exit strategies. Fil's approach prioritizes human dignity over monetization, building authentic word-of-mouth rather than aggressive scaling. The evolution from June to December 2025 shows a strategic pivot from "global disruption with $360M projections" to "helping Ginette call her daughter" - the passion remains ignited but highly focused. Fil follows a rigorous professional lab methodology with sequential validation phases P0‚ÜíP5, treating the assistant as a "GPS not autopilot" that anticipates obstacles and suggests alternatives while respecting the validation matrix. The project has completed P0 (Founder validation) and is positioned to begin P1 (Product Requirements Document with Product Owner). Current state Fil has completed an intensive 6-month learning journey, evolving from knowing nothing about ChatGPT in May 2025 to creating approximately 1,450 files across 17 prototypes by December 2025. Key technical achievements include a Trust Score Algorithm (1,750 lines), Family Guardian Network (1,217 lines), sophisticated voice systems, contextual laugh systems (48 files), and voice DNA profiles (56 files). The project has successfully completed Phase P0 (strategic validation) and all subsequent phases through P5: P1 (Product Requirements Document with 25 user stories), P2 (UX Research with 30-38 interviews and 10 ready testers), P3 (Technical Architecture with 15 ADRs and comprehensive technical specifications), P4 (Legal compliance under Quebec Law 25/PIPEDA with all required documentation), and P5 (Financial validation approving the minimal Alpha phase). The project is now ready to transition from planning to actual code development for the Alpha version. A critical architectural decision was made during P4 to use predefined mock screenshots instead of real user data during Alpha testing, dramatically reducing privacy risks while maintaining the ability to test conversational mechanics. The technical stack is established: FastAPI backend, SwiftUI iOS app, PostgreSQL database, deployed on Railway PaaS, with Claude 3.5 Sonnet as the primary LLM. Key learnings & principles The fundamental insight driving iAngel is that existing AI assistants optimize for speed while vulnerable users need safety and comprehension. The "one step at a time" methodology with explicit validation represents a complete inversion of typical AI UX patterns. Rather than providing overwhelming multi-step lists, iAngel gives single instructions and waits for user confirmation before proceeding. Fil discovered that the core innovation isn't in screenshot capture technology itself, but in the contextual questioning that happens between capture and API submission. By asking "What do you want to know about this capture?" before sending images to the AI backend, responses become targeted to actual user concerns rather than generic descriptions. The project's evolution demonstrates the power of focused iteration over broad ambition. The shift from global disruption goals to helping individual seniors represents strategic clarity rather than reduced vision. The principle of "GPS not autopilot" emerged as the optimal assistant relationship - anticipating obstacles and suggesting alternatives while respecting user agency and the structured validation process. Privacy by Design principles proved essential, with the predefined screenshot approach eliminating major compliance barriers while maintaining testing effectiveness. The realization that voice activation through Siri provides superior UX for seniors compared to visual buttons challenged conventional mobile app assumptions. Approach & patterns Fil employs a rigorous professional laboratory methodology with sequential phases P0 through P5, refusing to skip validation steps despite pressure to move quickly to implementation. Each phase requires formal deliverables and explicit approval before proceeding to the next stage. The development approach emphasizes YAGNI (You Aren't Gonna Need It) principles, choosing simple solutions over premature optimization. For the Alpha phase, this meant selecting Device ID authentication over complex auth systems, minimal monitoring over expensive APM tools, and REST APIs over streaming optimization. Documentation follows professional standards with comprehensive Architecture Decision Records (ADRs), formal compliance reports, and investor-ready strategic documents. Fil demonstrates preference for working through deliverables sequentially, requesting each document individually before moving to the next. The user research approach involved 30-38 interviews with 10 ready testers identified for Alpha deployment. All decisions are validated through the "Ginette lens" - ensuring interfaces and flows work for the archetypal 72-year-old user who fears fraud and avoids technical jargon. Tools & resources The technical stack centers on FastAPI (Python backend) with SwiftUI (iOS native), PostgreSQL database, and Railway PaaS for deployment. Claude 3.5 Sonnet serves as the primary LLM with multi-provider abstraction for future flexibility. The architecture includes comprehensive logging for learning from consenting Alpha testers. Development tools include Sentry for error monitoring, UptimeRobot for uptime tracking, and TestFlight for Alpha distribution to the 10 pilot testers. The approach emphasizes free/minimal cost tools appropriate for the Alpha scale rather than enterprise solutions. For compliance, Fil has completed all required Quebec Law 25/PIPEDA documentation including RPRP designation, privacy policy written in "Ginette-friendly" language, consent forms for Alpha testers, and Privacy Impact Assessment (EFVP) for data transfers to Anthropic in the United States. The project leverages extensive documentation using professional Word document generation with docx-js, creating formal deliverables that meet professional laboratory standards while maintaining accessibility for the target senior demographic.
